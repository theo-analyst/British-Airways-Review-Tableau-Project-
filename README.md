# British Airways Customer Review Analysis – Tableau Dashboard

## Business Problem
British Airways needs to understand customer satisfaction trends across different service categories, traveller types, and aircraft in order to improve customer experience and maintain brand loyalty.

Customer reviews contain valuable feedback, but without proper analysis it is difficult to identify patterns, weaknesses, and areas requiring immediate action.

## Objective
The goal of this project was to analyze customer review data and uncover actionable insights that can support operational and customer service decisions.

## Key Business Questions
This analysis answers questions such as:

- Which service categories receive the lowest ratings?
- How has customer satisfaction changed over time?
- Which traveller types are least satisfied?
- Which aircraft receive the highest and lowest ratings?
- Which countries or regions report the best and worst experiences?

## Data Analysis Process
To answer these questions, I:

- Cleaned and prepared the review dataset for analysis.
- Created calculated fields and KPIs in Tableau.
- Built interactive filters for deeper exploration.
- Visualized trends over time, by geography, and by aircraft type.
- Compared service metrics such as:
  - Cabin Staff Service
  - Entertainment
  - Food & Beverages
  - Ground Service
  - Seat Comfort
  - Value for Money

## Key Insights
From the analysis, I found that:

- Overall customer satisfaction fluctuates significantly over time.
- Some aircraft models consistently receive higher ratings than others.
- Certain countries report stronger customer experiences.
- Seat comfort and entertainment appear to be weaker-performing areas.

## Business Recommendations
Based on this analysis, British Airways should:

- Investigate underperforming aircraft and service categories.
- Improve seat comfort and entertainment services.
- Focus on traveller segments with lower satisfaction.
- Benchmark high-performing regions and replicate best practices.
